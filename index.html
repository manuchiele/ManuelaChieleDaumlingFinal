My Reasons To Live

I moved to the U.S. when I was 13 years old. At the age of 14, I started dealing with anxiety and a lack of motivation. By 16, I was depressed and barely left my room. I isolated myself from everyone I knew, including myself. I repressed parts of myself that I'm only able to access now that I'm almost 20 years old. In the last two years, I've been looking for reasons to keep going and trying to find beauty in life again. The best thing I found was taking pictures of things I find beautiful. I have a private Instagram where I share these pictures with my closest friends. I post pictures for every single day I've lived since I started this little project. I have 732 posts at the moment. I can't imagine who I'd be if I hadn't started this Instagram; it helped me a lot, especially in the darkest days. I take pictures of anything I find pretty, from leaves on the ground to my cat bathing in the sunâ€”anything goes. It really makes you pay attention and notice how life is happening around you. I wish everyone could look at life as if they were a child still learning the names of things; it is so much brighter and more exciting. On this website, I will share some of my favorite pictures that I've taken so far. I hope you enjoy and appreciate them as much as I do.

Reasons to Take Pictures of Pretty Things:

  • Appreciation of Life's Beauty:
    Taking pictures of pretty things allows you to actively seek and appreciate the beauty that surrounds you, fostering a positive outlook on life.

  • Mindfulness and Presence:
    Photography encourages you to be present in the moment, focusing on the details of your surroundings and promoting mindfulness.

  • Builds a Visual Diary:
    Documenting pretty things creates a visual diary of your life, capturing moments and scenes that bring you joy. This can be a source of reflection and nostalgia in the future.

  • Encourages Exploration:
    Seeking out beautiful subjects for photography encourages exploration of your surroundings. It motivates you to discover new places and appreciate the diversity in the world.

  • Connection with Others:
    Sharing your photographs, whether on social media or with friends and family, can create connections. It allows you to share your experiences and perspectives, fostering a sense of community.

  • Boosts Mood and Well-Being:
    Engaging with beauty through photography has the potential to boost your mood and overall sense of well-being. It provides a visual reminder of the positive aspects of life.
